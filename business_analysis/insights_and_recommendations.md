# Business Insights & Recommendations

## Executive Summary

From the analysis, it is clear that the business mainly grows through **volume**, not pricing.

Revenue is strongly driven by the number of **customers and orders**, while metrics like AOV (basket size) don’t contribute much — and can even have a negative effect.

At the same time, the contribution margin stays very stable (~46.6%) across all months. This tells us that pricing and cost structure are consistent, and that changes in revenue are mostly driven by **demand**, not efficiency.

The sharp drop in December is most likely not a margin issue, but rather a **drop in activity or incomplete data**.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Growth comes from customers and orders

Revenue is highly correlated with:
- Orders (~0.98)
- Active customers (~0.97)

This confirms that the business is clearly **volume-driven**.

==> More customers = more orders = more revenue

---

### 2. AOV is not a strong driver

AOV shows a negative correlation (~ -0.28).

From a business perspective, this suggests that:
- pushing customers to spend more per order may reduce conversion
- large baskets might create friction

 ==>In this case, increasing basket size is not the best growth strategy

---

### 3. Margins are very stable

Contribution margin stays around **46.6% every month**.

This means:
- pricing is consistent
- cost structure is stable
- no major efficiency issues

==> Revenue changes are not coming from pricing, but from demand

---

### 4. Profit depends on revenue and marketing

From the profit bridge:
- profit is mainly driven by **revenue changes**
- and also impacted by **marketing spend**

Margin itself does not change much.

==> This suggests that growth is partly being "bought" through marketing

---

### 5. December drop is not a margin issue

Even though revenue drops a lot in December:
- margin stays stable

==> This likely means:
- fewer customers / orders
- possible missing data
- or reduced activity

---

## Business Interpretation

The company is operating as a **volume-first business**.

Growth depends on:
- acquiring more customers
- increasing number of orders

Since margins are stable, the main challenge is not pricing — it is how efficiently the business can grow and sustain demand.

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Focus on retention (not only acquisition)

Since growth depends on volume, it’s important to:
- bring customers back
- increase repeat purchases

=> Actions:
- email / CRM campaigns
- loyalty programs
- personalized follow-ups

---

### 2. Be careful with AOV optimization

Trying to increase basket size too much may:
- reduce conversion
- slow down purchases

==> Better focus on:
- frequency (how often customers buy)
- not just how much they spend

---

### 3. Improve marketing efficiency

Profit is sensitive to marketing spend.

==> So the goal should be:
- reduce inefficient campaigns
- focus on high-ROI channels
- balance acquisition vs retention

---

### 4. Investigate the December drop

This is important.

==> Need to check:
- if data is complete
- if campaigns stopped
- if demand dropped (seasonality)

---

## Final Conclusion

This is a **volume-driven e-commerce business** with stable margins.

Growth is predictable, but depends heavily on:
- customer acquisition
- order volume
- marketing efficiency

==> The main opportunity is to improve **retention and efficiency**, not pricing.
